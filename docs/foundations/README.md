## The one finding that matters most
**Artificial Analysis hit our exact problem and solved it by *replacing* saturated
benchmarks, not reweighting.** Their Intelligence Index **v4.0** (Dec 2025) explicitly dropped
MMLU-Pro, AIME 2025, and LiveCodeBench because the frontier had compressed them; the top score
fell from **73 → 50**. The industry leader's answer to our exact failure is precedent for ours.

## My reconciliation (Claude's read)

The synthesis proposed a **5-axis suite** (Knowledge&Reasoning 25% / Math 20% / Instruction-
Following 20% / Agentic 20% / Coding 15%). The red-team is **right** that, as weighted, it
repeats v0:

- **2 clean wins** — adopt with confidence:
  - **SuperGPQA** (knowledge): open ODC-BY where GPQA is gated; post-cutoff (contamination-
    resistant); discriminates across the *local* range (7B ~30% → R1 62%). The standout pick.
  - **IFBench** (instruction-following): built *because* IFEval saturated; programmatic
    verifiers (no judge); AA confirms it hasn't saturated in 6 months.
- **3 at-risk** — do NOT ship as weighted core on current evidence:
  - **CRUXEval-O** (coding): actually saturated — GPT-4 already ~82%. A 2024 code-trace task.
  - **BFCL-AST** (agentic): Berkeley themselves re-weighted V4 *away* from it for saturating.
  - **MathArena fresh/Apex** (math): the final-answer-competition format was declared dead at
    the frontier on 2026-05-12 (GPT-5.5 solved the last Apex problem).

**The decisive lesson: we cannot pick or weight benchmarks by reading leaderboards — they go
stale in weeks. We must measure anchor spread on our OWN runs and let measured discrimination
drive selection AND weights.** This is the validity gate P0 never had (P0 validated
repeatability + licensing, never discrimination). The scoring stack itself is sound — this is a
*content* swap, not a math rewrite.

**Scope reframe that dissolves half the tension:** the product's job is to separate a 9B from a
32B from frontier — the **local range** — not to rank frontier models against each other.
Anchors are a *reference ceiling*. So an axis only needs to (a) discriminate across the local
range and (b) keep the frontier anchors clearly *above* the locals (which is exactly what v0
genmath failed). Frontier-vs-frontier separation is likely out of reach for a self-hostable
suite, and that's OK if stated honestly.

## Recommended next step (needs sign-off — involves anchor spend + local GPU)
A **discrimination probe** before committing any suite/weights:
1. Lock the 2 proven axes (SuperGPQA, IFBench) + keep the existing scoring stack.
2. Run the 4 anchors + 2-3 local models (the Qwen 9B + a small 1-3B) on a sample of each
   candidate bench. Compute per-bench floor→frontier spread. **Keep/weight only benches where
   the spread is real; drop or demote the rest.** Ship FEWER, genuinely-discriminating axes.
3. Be honest about what's unmeasurable locally (frontier-vs-frontier; real coding ability needs
   exec/Docker) — label those "reported context", never charted on a measured axis.
